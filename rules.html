Table Tennis Rules:

All rules have been paraphrased for your convenience from the official table tennis handbook. For official rules, go here.
  • A serve must be tossed up vertically 6 inches from an open palm.
  • A serve cannot be spun directly with the hand.
  • A serve must be behind the edge of the table and above the surface of the table.
  • The ball must be visible to the opponent at all times during the serve.
  • A serve must strike the server’s side, bounce over the net, and fall onto the opponent’s side.
  • The ball must hit returner’s side of the table before it can be returned back to the opponent’s side of the table.
  • A let, or re-do, occurs when:
    • A serve touches the net but is otherwise a valid serve.
    • The opposing player is not ready.
    • The umpire believes there is an error.
  • Points are scored when:
    • A let has not occurred.
    • A correct serve is failed.
    • A correct return is failed.
  • Points go until 11, but a deuce occurs when both players reach 10 points.
  • A deuce is when a player must score 2 more than their opponent to win, and occurs until both players reach 18 points or 10 minutes have past, when the expedite system is initialized for the current match and subsequent matches.

Ranking Calculations:

  • Players start at 50 points.
  • 10 = base point change for equal players, meaning 30 point difference between the players.
  • Minimum of 5 points gained from a match.
  • Maximum point change formula = point difference between two players / 2.
    • Applied when the point difference is at least 50.
    • Does not apply to streak bonuses.
  • Win/Lose Streaks:
    • Bonus/Protection given at a streak of at least 3.
    • Win streak bonus formula = 2 * number of wins.
    • Loss streak protection formula = points lost * (1 - .05 * number of losses).
    • Win streak bonuses give you extra points, and loss streak protection gives cushion to loss of points.
  • Point change formulas for matches with players with significant point difference:
    • Formula if the lower point player wins = base point change + point difference * .3; active when there is more than a 30 point difference
    • Formula if the higher point player wins = base point change - (point difference - 50) / 10; active when there is more than a 60 point difference
